What is PCI DSS Audit?

PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard) is a globally recognized security framework that establishes mandatory requirements for protecting cardholder data during its processing, storage, and transmission. It applies to all entities involved in payment card transactions and defines the technical and operational controls necessary to reduce fraud, strengthen data security, and maintain the integrity of the payment ecosystem.

Why PCI DSS Matters

Any organization that processes, stores, or transmits payment card data is responsible for protecting it. PCI DSS provides a comprehensive security framework that builds every layer of the payment environment from network controls to monitoring, access governance, and incident response.

PCI DSS Benefits

PCI DSS strengthens cybersecurity posture and protects sensitive cardholder data, ensuring secure payment environments.

Demonstrate Accountability to Customers & Partners

Strong security controls reinforce customer confidence, support brand credibility, and strengthen trust across the payment ecosystem.

Risk of Non-Compliance

Penalty fees, legal liabilities, reputational damage, investigation charges, and possible suspension of card processing may occur if PCI DSS is ignored.

PCI DSS Requirements (12 Core Controls)

1. Install & Maintain Network Security Controls

Deploy firewalls, segmentation, and secure architecture to protect cardholder data paths.

2. Apply Secure Configurations

Harden system components using standardized security baselines.

3. Protect Stored Account Data

Use tokenization, hashing, truncation, and encryption to secure stored cardholder data.

4. Secure Transmission of Cardholder Data

Protect transmitted data using strong encryption like TLS 1.2+.

5. Protect Against Malware

Implement anti-malware solutions and continuous monitoring.

6. Maintain Secure Systems & Software

Perform updates, vulnerability remediation, and secure SDLC practices.

7. Restrict Access by Need-to-Know

Ensure only authorized staff can access cardholder data.

8. Identify & Authenticate Access

Use MFA, strong authentication, and secure credential management.

9. Restrict Physical Access

Secure data centers and areas where CHD is stored or processed.

10. Log & Monitor Access

Enable centralized logging, monitoring, and anomaly detection.

11. Test Security Regularly

Perform scans, penetration testing, and integrity checks.

12. Maintain a Security Policy

Implement ongoing risk management, documentation, and security awareness training.

PCI DSS Compliance Levels

Level 1

6 Million+ Transactions / Year

  • ✔ QSA Audit
  • ✔ Annual ROC
  • ✔ Quarterly ASV Scans
Level 2

1–6 Million Transactions / Year

  • ✔ Annual SAQ
  • ✔ Possible ASV Scans
Level 3

20,000–1 Million eCommerce Transactions

  • ✔ Annual SAQ
  • ✔ Quarterly ASV Scans
Level 4

<20,000 eCommerce or <1M In-Person Transactions

  • ✔ Annual SAQ
  • ✔ ASV Scans (If Required)

PCI DSS SAQs (Self-Assessment Questionnaires)

Select the appropriate SAQ based on your business model and payment environment.

SAQ A

Fully outsourced e-commerce or card-not-present environments.

SAQ A-EP

For e-commerce with partial payment handling.

SAQ B

For imprint devices or dial-out terminals.

SAQ B-IP

For IP-connected standalone terminals.

SAQ C

Internet-connected payment applications.

SAQ C-VT

For web-based virtual terminal environments.

SAQ D (Merchants)

For full PCI scope environments.

SAQ D (Service Providers)

For companies processing sensitive card data.

SAQ P2PE

For validated P2PE environments.

SAQ SPoC

For secure software-based PIN entry.

Each SAQ aligns with unique PCI DSS validation pathways.

Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to the most common PCI DSS compliance queries.

1. How often are PCI DSS audits required? + −
PCI DSS requires an annual audit or Self-Assessment Questionnaire (SAQ) submission. Quarterly vulnerability scans are also mandatory.
2. What happens if we fail a PCI DSS audit? + −
Failing PCI DSS may lead to fines, additional audits, increased processing fees, mandatory remediation, or loss of the ability to accept card payments.
3. Who needs PCI DSS compliance? + −
Any business—regardless of size—that stores, processes, or transmits cardholder data.
4. Is PCI DSS legally mandatory? + −
PCI DSS is enforced by card brands and acquiring banks. While not a law, it is contractually required for accepting card transactions in most regions.
