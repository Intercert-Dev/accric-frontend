Climate Change Management

Carbon Footprinting

Measures the total greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions caused directly and indirectly by an organization or product. It helps businesses identify emission sources, set reduction targets, and implement sustainable practices.

Carbon & GHG Emissions Verification & Reporting (ISO 14064)

Ensures organizations accurately report their emissions and comply with climate regulations. It provides a standardized framework for measuring, verifying, and reducing carbon footprints.

GRI Consulting & Reporting

Uses Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) Standards to enhance sustainability disclosures and transparency. It helps organizations communicate their environmental, social, and governance (ESG) performance to stakeholders effectively.

Energy Management

ISO 50001

A framework to establish, implement, maintain, and improve an energy management system (EnMS) to enhance energy efficiency and reduce costs. It helps organizations systematically optimize energy use, reduce environmental impact, and comply with energy regulations.

Environment Management

ISO 14001

Provides guidelines for an effective environmental management system (EMS) that helps organizations minimize their environmental footprint and comply with regulations. It focuses on sustainable resource use, waste reduction, pollution prevention, and continuous environmental performance improvement.

Health & Safety Management

ISO 45001

Sets standards for workplace health and safety, reducing risks, preventing injuries, and ensuring worker well-being. It provides a proactive framework for identifying hazards, implementing safety measures, and fostering a culture of occupational health and safety compliance.

Corporate Social Responsibility

ISO 26001

Offers guidelines on social responsibility, including ethical business practices, community engagement, and sustainable development. It helps organizations integrate social, environmental, and governance (ESG) principles into their operations to create positive societal impact.

Sustainable Management

ISO 37101

Focuses on sustainability management for communities and cities, promoting resilience and environmental balance. It helps urban areas implement sustainable development strategies while enhancing quality of life.

ISO 53001

Supports the integration of sustainable practices in organizations for long-term business and environmental stability. It provides a structured approach to embedding sustainability into corporate decision-making and operations.

ISO 20121

Establishes sustainability standards for event management, ensuring responsible resource use and minimal environmental impact. It helps organizations plan and execute events that align with social, economic, and environmental sustainability goals.

FAQS

1. What is Climate Change Management, and why is it important? + −
Climate Change Management involves strategies like carbon footprinting, greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions verification, and sustainability reporting to mitigate environmental impact. It is crucial for reducing global warming, complying with regulations, and achieving corporate sustainability goals. .
2. What is ISO 14064, and how does it relate to carbon and GHG emissions reporting? + −
ISO 14064 is an international standard for quantifying, monitoring, and reporting greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions. It helps organizations track their carbon footprint, implement reduction strategies, and ensure transparency in sustainability efforts.
3. How does ISO 50001 help with Energy Management? + −
ISO 50001 provides a framework for organizations to improve energy performance, reduce consumption, and enhance efficiency. Implementing this standard leads to cost savings, reduced carbon footprint, and compliance with energy regulations.
4. What is Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) under ISO 26001? + −
ISO 26001 provides guidance on Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR), helping businesses integrate social, environmental, and ethical responsibilities into their operations. It ensures sustainable business practices and positive community engagement.
5. What are the key benefits of implementing Sustainable Management standards like ISO 37101 and ISO 20121? + −
Sustainable Management standards promote responsible resource use, environmental protection, and long-term business viability. ISO 37101 focuses on sustainable development for communities, while ISO 20121 ensures sustainability in event management.
